## üåä EXPERT COASTAL LANDSCAPING ADVICE

### Common Coastal Challenges & Solutions

**Problem: Salt Spray Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Brown, burned foliage; stunted growth; dead branches
- **Solutions:**
  - Plant salt-tolerant species (Rosa rugosa, Bayberry, Beach Plum)
  - Install windbreaks (fencing, shrub barriers)
  - Rinse plants after storms with fresh water
  - Apply anti-desiccant sprays in winter
  - Choose native coastal plants (naturally adapted)

**Problem: Sandy Soil Lacks Nutrients**
- **Symptoms:** Yellow grass, poor plant growth, rapid fertilizer leaching
- **Solutions:**
  - Add organic matter (compost, peat moss) annually
  - Use slow-release fertilizers
  - Mulch heavily (3-4") to retain moisture and nutrients
  - Plant nitrogen-fixing plants (Bayberry, Clover)
  - More frequent, lighter fertilization

**Problem: Wind Damage and Erosion**
- **Symptoms:** Soil blowing away, plants toppling, landscape degradation
- **Solutions:**
  - Deep-rooted native plantings (Beach Grass, Goldenrod)
  - Windbreaks and living fences
  - Ground covers to hold soil
  - Hardscaping for permanent stabilization
  - Strategic grading and berming

**Problem: Flooding and Drainage Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Standing water, foundation concerns, plant die-off
- **Solutions:**
  - French drains and catch basins
  - Regrading to improve water flow
  - Rain gardens for water absorption
  - Raised planting beds
  - Permeable paving materials

**Problem: Drought Stress in Summer**
- **Symptoms:** Brown grass, wilting plants, dry soil
- **Solutions:**
  - Drought-tolerant plant selection
  - Efficient irrigation systems (drip, soaker hoses)
  - Deep watering less frequently (encourages deep roots)
  - Organic mulch to retain moisture
  - Reduce turf areas (less water needed)
